History Of Coronavirus:

  • Human coronaviruses were discovered in the 1960s.
  • Two novel strains B814 and 229E were subsequently imaged by electron microscopy in 1967 by Scottish virologist June Almeida in London.
  • This new group of IBV-like viruses which were shown to be morphologically related to the mouse hepatitis virus, came to be known as coronaviruses.
  • Other human coronaviruses have since been identified, including SARS-CoV in 2003, HCoV HKU1 in 2005, MERS-CoV in 2012.
  • There have also been a large number of animal coronaviruses identified since the 1960s.

What is a coronavirus?

  • Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses which may cause illness in animals or humans.
  • In humans, several coronaviruses are known to cause respiratory infections ranging from the common cold to more severe diseases such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS) and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS).
  • The most recently discovered coronavirus causes coronavirus disease COVID-19.

What is COVID-19?

  • COVID-19 is the infectious disease caused by the most recently discovered coronavirus.
  • This new virus and disease were unknown before the outbreak began in Wuhan, China, in December 2019.
  • COVID-19 is now a pandemic affecting many countries globally.
